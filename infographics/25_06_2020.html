How does Times of India report?: A textual analysis

  1. Back to Infographics
  2. Published on 25 June, 2020

How do we infer?

To see what how does Times of India structures the headlines of its reports, we tried to do a basic text analytics on all the headlines scraped from the start of this year. We used Spacy for tokenising and NER tagging.

Summary

We see on an average the headline is composed of mostly 9 words. POS tagging reveals common nouns and proper nouns are mostly used for structuring sentences in the headlines

Popular Keywords - 'Indian States'

We see Delhi and Maharashtra were the most talked Indian states in this period. Interestingly, China and US were most talked even more than Pakistan. In popular personalities, Modi , Rahul Gandhi, Yogi Aditynath were the frontrunners.

Popular Keywords - 'Countries'

Interestingly, China and US were most talked even more than Pakistan.

Popular Keywords - 'Personalities & Orgs'

In popular personalities, Modi , Rahul Gandhi, Yogi Aditynath were the frontrunners.

t-SNE Clustering of (2-words) LDA & LSA Topics (top-8)

Forming buckets of topics for the headlines, we use two pretty interesting algorithms namely, LSA and LDA. Some of the topics like 'covid 19', 'delhi elections', 'coronavirus lockdown' makes sense as these were the most talked about points throughout the year. When each document is represented in this space, we do a fast Approximate Nearest Neighbour search using t-SNE (t-distributed stochastic neighbor embedding) algorithm, and cluster all documents that are within a certain distance from each other. Using a distance based metric has an added advantage of being able to capture near and exact duplicates. The documents that are mapped too close to each other are considered to be the same story. The documents that are a certain radius away from the exact duplicates make up the roundups for each story.
