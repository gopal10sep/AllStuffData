Consumer Price Index

When there is a change in prices, it affects the quantity of goods and services that can be purchased by a fixed income. Researchers take into account both the amount that can be bought with a unit of currency and the changes in item quality when measuring changes in prices. To understand the overall changes in prices for a specific group such as consumers or manufacturers, researchers create a representative basket of relevant goods and services for the group and track the changes in the basket and its price over time. This results in a price index. The rate of inflation can then be calculated using the price index. One of the most frequently used methods of measuring inflation is to determine the 12-month percent change in the index values, which shows how prices in a particular month compare to prices from the same month one year earlier.



Inflation Expectations

Researchers acquire information on probable variations in prices by conducting frequent surveys of people and analyzing market information. One method based on market trends is called the inflation breakeven and is determined by comparing the return on a standard government bond to the return on a bond that is protected against inflation, both with the same maturity. The gap between these two yields represents the average level of inflation that the market anticipates for the duration of the bond. From FED: The breakeven inflation rate represents a measure of expected inflation derived from 5-Year Treasury Constant Maturity Securities (BC_5YEAR) and 5-Year Treasury Inflation-Indexed Constant Maturity Securities (TC_5YEAR). The latest value implies what market participants expect inflation to be in the next 5 years, on average.
